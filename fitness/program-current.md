## Overview

Running is managed by Runna. This program covers **strength only** — 3 × full body sessions per week, 20 min each. Every session hits every muscle group. Lower body pattern rotates (squat → hinge → single-leg) so the same muscles aren't maxed out every day.

**Why full body over a split:**
With 20-min sessions, a split gives each muscle group only ~2–3 sets once per week. Full body gives ~2–3 sets three times per week at the same or lower weekly time investment. Higher frequency = faster skill acquisition (important as a beginner) and resilience against missed sessions.

**Equipment:** 2× DBs up to 25kg, resistance bands

## Superset logic

- **Lower compound + core** → superset: lower body rests while core works, no performance compromise
- **Upper push + upper pull** → superset: perfect antagonists, one fully recovers while the other works
- **Plyometrics** → always standalone with full rest: explosive work needs full neural readiness
- **Net saving:** ~4–5 min per session vs. straight sets

## Session 1 — Squat pattern (Monday)

**Lower focus:** Goblet squat

### Block A — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**A1: Goblet Squat** — 8–10 reps
> Hold DB at chest, feet shoulder-width, pause 1s at bottom to open hips. Heels elevated on plates/books if needed.

*20s transition*

**A2: Dead Bug** — 6 reps/side
> Lower back pressed to floor. Extend opposite arm + leg slowly. Exhale as you extend. Core, not momentum.

*45s rest → back to A1*

---

### Block B — Plyometrics (standalone) × 2 rounds
**B: Jump Squat** — 6–8 reps
> Squat to parallel, explode up, land soft through the full foot. Reset fully between reps — these are not bounces.

*75s rest*

---

### Block C — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**C1: DB Floor Press** — 10–12 reps
> Neutral or semi-neutral grip. Elbows ~45° from torso. Full ROM limited by floor — that's fine, protects shoulders.

*20s transition*

**C2: DB Bent-over Row** — 10–12 reps/arm (or both arms)
> Hinge to ~45°, row to hip. Squeeze at top. Keep back flat.

*60s rest → back to C1*

---

### Block D — Finisher
**Band Hip Circle** — 12 reps each direction
> Band around ankles, slight squat, circles. Hip stabilizer activation. No rest — flow straight through.

---

## Session 2 — Hinge pattern (Wednesday)

**Lower focus:** Romanian Deadlift

### Block A — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**A1: DB Romanian Deadlift** — 8–10 reps
> Hip hinge — push hips back, soft knee, feel the hamstring stretch. DBs stay close to legs. Drive hips forward to stand.

*20s transition*

**A2: Side Plank (hip abduction hold)** — 20–30s/side
> Straight line head to heel. Top hip doesn't sag. Can add hip dip for extra work.

*45s rest → back to A1*

---

### Block B — Plyometrics (standalone) × 2 rounds
**B: Step-up Jump** — 6 reps/leg
> Step up onto a chair/box/step, drive the opposite knee up explosively at the top. Land controlled. Alternate legs or do all reps one side then switch.

*75s rest*

---

### Block C — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**C1: DB Seated Overhead Press** — 10–12 reps
> Seated (more stable, better for shoulder mobility). Start light. Only press through comfortable range — no forcing overhead if it's not there yet.

*20s transition*

**C2: Band Pull-apart** — 15 reps
> Arms straight at shoulder height, pull band apart to chest. Slow eccentric. Shoulder health — non-negotiable.

*60s rest → back to C1*

---

### Block D — Finisher
**90/90 Hip Stretch** — 45s each side
> Front leg and back leg both at 90°. Sit tall. This directly addresses your hip rotation mobility. Hold, breathe, don't force.

---

## Session 3 — Single-leg pattern (Friday)

**Lower focus:** Single-leg RDL

### Block A — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**A1: DB Single-leg RDL** — 8 reps/leg
> One DB, same-side or opposite hand. Hinge with control. Balance is a skill — don't rush it in week 1. Touch a wall if needed.

*20s transition*

**A2: Pallof Press (band)** — 8 reps/side
> Band anchored at door/table leg at chest height. Press out and hold 2s. Core anti-rotation — trains the obliques in their actual running function.

*45s rest → back to A1*

---

### Block B — Plyometrics (standalone) × 2 rounds
**B: Broad Jump** — 5 reps
> Two-foot takeoff, jump forward for distance, land soft in a squat. Most running-economy-specific plyo. Full reset between reps.

*75s rest*

---

### Block C — Superset × 2–3 rounds
**C1: DB Floor Press (incline if possible)** — 10–12 reps
> Same as Monday but try slight incline (backpack under upper back) for variety if comfortable.

*20s transition*

**C2: 1-arm DB Row** — 10 reps/arm
> Brace on knee or bench. Full shoulder blade retraction at top.

*60s rest → back to C1*

---

### Block D — Finisher
**World's Greatest Stretch** — 3 reps/side, slow
> Lunge position: front foot flat, back knee down. Rotate toward front leg, reach overhead. Addresses hip flexors AND shoulder simultaneously. Your two mobility targets in one move.

## Notes from coach

**2026-04-09 — Redesigned as full body**
Switched from A/B/C split to 3× full body. With 20-min sessions, full body gives more weekly sets per muscle group and more movement practice per week — both important at beginner stage. Lower body pattern rotates across sessions (squat/hinge/single-leg) to avoid repeating the same pattern 3x/week.

Supersets are paired as: lower + core (saves ~2 min/session) and push + pull (saves ~2 min/session). Plyometrics remain standalone — explosive work needs full neural recovery.

Start with 2 sets in week 1. If 20 min feels tight, drop Block D to just 1 set — mobility finisher is a bonus, not the main event.

First week priority: feel out your RPE baseline. Log the weight and how hard it felt. We calibrate from there.
